Authenticity in Effect

Part 2: Why being real is more than just a vibe.

By Kranthi Erra • May 24, 2025 • Culture & Faith

One thing that always stops me when I am scrolling through social media is the "Relatability Performance." I see influencers trying so hard to appear like "people’s people," but there’s a disconnect.

Just this afternoon, I was watching a documentary with Will Smith traveling through Bhutan. It seemed like he was trying to win our hearts back by exploring happiness, but he was doing it with cameras recording his every move. He was trying so hard to be "new" while remaining the same old person.

"The world’s authenticity is about affecting how people see us; Kingdom authenticity is about having an effect on the world around us."

We live in a generation that has a built-in "fake detector." We understand what is real and what is a curated aesthetic. This brings us back to the story of the Samaritan woman in John 4.

"Jesus said to her, 'Go, call your husband, and come here.' The woman answered him, 'I have no husband.' Jesus said to her, 'You are right... for you have had five husbands... What you have said is true.'"
John 4:16–18

Point 1: No-Filter

When she chose to be authentic, Jesus didn't shame her. He affirmed her. I love this because it shows us that Jesus isn't looking for a polished version of our lives; He is looking for the "No-Filter" version.

This is Authenticity in Effect: When we stop trying to impress God and simply get honest with Him, the immediate result is that He reveals His power to us.

Point 2: More Than a Vibe

"God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth."
John 4:24

The moment Jesus points out her mess, she gets defensive and tries to start a religious argument about mountains. She’s trying to use religion as a filter. But Jesus tells her that the "where" doesn't matter anymore.

We often think of worship as a "vibe"—lighting, songs, or buildings. But Jesus says worship is about Internal Reality, not External Performance. You cannot worship in "Truth" if you are wearing a mask.

Point 3: The Chain Reaction

It started with one tiny, "No-Filter" moment. That one ball dropping hit the next domino—she told the town, and the whole community shifted. They said: "It is no longer because of what you said that we believe, for we have heard for ourselves."

Our masks block the dominoes from falling. Authenticity in Effect starts a chain reaction that reaches people you haven't even met yet.

Final Thoughts: Removing the Labels

We often label people by their struggles. But Jesus looked past the label to the human being underneath. He removed the labels that kept them in "social cages."

  • The Leper ("The Untouchable")
    (Matt. 8:1–4)
    He was a social outcast. Jesus didn’t just clear his skin; He ended his loneliness and made him a neighbor again.
  • The Blind Beggar ("The Burden")
    (Mark 10:46–53)
    Trapped by economic constraint. Jesus didn't just give him sight; He gave him independence.
  • The Hemorrhaging Woman ("The Taboo")
    (Mark 5:25–34)
    For twelve years, she was excluded. Jesus called her "Daughter" and made her welcome again.

A Closing Prayer

Heavenly Father, thank You that You do not see us through the labels this world tries to stick on us. We admit that sometimes we have lived in 'social cages.' We thank You for Jesus, who stepped past every barrier to call us neighbors. Today, give us the courage to drop our masks. Help us to live authentically, not to win the hearts of the world, but to reflect Your heart. In Jesus' name, Amen.
