Encountering Authenticity

Exploring the story of Jesus and the Samaritan woman through the lens of modern rivalries.

By Kranthi Erra • January 22, 2026 • Reflection

I am not originally from Oswego, New York. But since moving here, people have told me there is a long-standing rivalry between the East Side and the West Side of town. Two thousand years ago, there was a rivalry much deeper: Jews vs. Samaritans. Jews saw Samaritans as "Opps"—the opposition.

In a time like that, Jesus stops at a well in a town of Samaritans. He knew exactly who would be there: a woman drawing water, in desperate need of something more than just a drink.

I can relate to this woman’s position. I was born in a Christian home in South India where our religion was a minority and often mistreated. More than that, I was born into a very low caste. In that society, asking for a drink was out of the question.

"Jesus said to her, 'Give me a drink.' The Samaritan woman said to him, 'How is it that you, a Jew, ask for a drink from me, a woman of Samaria?'"
— John 4:1-9

The Divine Detour

Most Jews would take a long route to avoid Samaria. But the Bible says Jesus "had to" pass through. To reach her, He broke every rule in the book:

  • The Route: He chose the town everyone avoided.
  • The Stop: He rested in a forbidden place.
  • The Water: He was willing to drink from an "unclean" vessel.
  • The Person: He started a conversation with a lone woman.

Choosing Connection Over Conflict

Jesus wasn't just breaking rules; He was offering salvation. In our politically and culturally divided time, the grass is very dry. A small spark can start a massive fire. But we are called to be the water, not the spark.

Dropping the Mask

The woman tried to deflect with theological debates ("Are you greater than Jacob?"). She was using "the wells of history" to avoid talking about her own life. But eventually, she realized she was tired of her routine. She dropped the mask and simply said: "Please, give me that water."

Reflection

Is there a "bridge" in your life you've been hesitant to cross? Let your transformation point to Jesus. Choose connection over conflict, and let His living water satisfy your thirst today.
