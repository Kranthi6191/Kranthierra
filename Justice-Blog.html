Why does the Bible emphasize the theme of justice so much?

Posted by: Kranthi Erra | Date: May 11, 2025 | Category: Faith & Reflection

Welcome to my little corner of the internet where I share my thoughts and reflections. Today, I wanted to talk about something very close to my heart: my ongoing journey in faith and the small, everyday moments where I find profound peace and connection.

It hasn't always been a straightforward path. Like many, I've had my share of questions, doubts, and times when the noise of the world seemed to drown out that quiet, guiding voice. But through it all, I've learned to cherish the practices and realizations that bring me back to center.

The Stillness of Morning

One of my most treasured times is the early morning. Before the day rushes in with its demands, there's a unique stillness. I find that starting my day with a simple prayer, a few verses of scripture, or even just quiet meditation can set a peaceful tone for everything that follows. It's in these moments, when the world is still asleep, that I feel closest to God and most receptive to His presence.

  • Waking up a little earlier than necessary.
  • Finding a comfortable, quiet spot.
  • Reading a devotional or scripture that resonates.
  • Spending a few minutes in silent prayer or gratitude.

Nature as a Cathedral

I've also found immense spiritual solace in nature. Whether it's a walk in a local park, tending to my small garden, or simply observing the clouds drift by, the intricate beauty of creation never fails to fill me with awe. It reminds me of the Creator's artistry and the interconnectedness of all things. Romans 1:20 often comes to mind: "For since the creation of the world God’s invisible qualities—his eternal power and divine nature—have been clearly seen, being understood from what has been made, so that people are without excuse."

"The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands." - Psalm 19:1

Community and Connection

Finally, sharing this journey with a community of believers has been invaluable. Discussing faith, supporting one another through challenges, and celebrating joys together strengthens my own convictions and provides a wonderful sense of belonging. It's a reminder that we are not meant to walk this path alone.

These are just a few personal reflections. I hope they might resonate with some of you, or perhaps inspire you to find your own moments of peace and connection in your faith journey. I'd love to hear your thoughts in the comments if this were an interactive blog!
